Beef Tapa
View recipe source here: Beef Tapa
Ingredients
300 g Beef Sirloin - cut into thin slices
3 tbsp Soy Sauce
3 tbsp Calamansi or Lemon juice
1 tbsp Garlic - finely minced
1/8 tsp Ground pepper
1/2 tsp Sugar
1/4 tsp Baking soda (optional)
1 bottle Oil - for frying
Instructions
  1. Using a mallet or any blunt object, pound each cut of meat until meat is flattened to about a quarter inch thick.
  2. Place the beef into a bowl and sprinkle with the baking soda. Mix until well blended.
  3. Add lemon juice and mix again several times. Then add the rest of the ingredients.
  4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and marinate beef in the fridge overnight.
  5. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a large pan or skillet. Fry each side of the meat for 3-5 minutes or until browned and the liquids are absorbed. Add more oil as needed.
  6. Serve with sunny-side-up egg and garlic fried rice while still hot.

Champorado
View recipe source here: Champorado
Ingredients
200 g Glutinous Rice
1 liter Water
50 g Cocoa Powder
100 g Sugar
1 pinch Salt
250 ml Evaporated Milk or Condensed Milk (for topping)
Instructions
  1. In a pot, bring the water to a boil. Add the glutinous rice and stir to prevent sticking.
  2. Reduce heat to medium and continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the mixture thickens.
  3. Dissolve cocoa powder in a small amount of hot water to make a paste. Add it to the rice mixture and stir well.
  4. Add sugar and a pinch of salt. Mix until well combined. Adjust sweetness to your preference.
  5. Serve hot in bowls, drizzled with evaporated or condensed milk on top.

Lucban Longganisa
View recipe source here: Lucban Longganisa
Ingredients
1 kg Ground Pork Belly (lean meat)
4 tbsp Garlic, finely minced
2 tbsp Fresh Oregano, minced
2 tbsp Paprika
2 tbsp Sugar
2 tbsp Salt
2 tbsp Ground Black Pepper
60 ml Vinegar
Sufficient quantity Pork Casings (optional)
Instructions
  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, minced garlic, minced oregano, paprika, sugar, salt, ground black pepper, and vinegar. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the mixture marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or overnight for better flavor.
  3. If using pork casings, soak them in warm water to soften, then rinse thoroughly. Stuff the marinated meat mixture into the casings and tie off at desired lengths. If making skinless longganisa, shape the meat mixture into logs about 4 inches (10 cm) long and 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick.
  4. Arrange the longganisa in a container, separating layers with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Freeze for at least 3 hours or until firm.
  5. To cook, place the longganisa in a pan with about 1/2 cup (120 ml) of water. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer until the water evaporates. Once the pan is dry, add a little oil and fry the sausages until browned and fully cooked.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice or fried rice, and enjoy!

Chicken and Pork Adobo
View recipe source here: Chicken and Pork Adobo
Ingredients
700 g Pork Belly, cut into 2-inch cubes
700 g Chicken, cut into serving pieces
1 tbsp Canola Oil
1 Onion, peeled and sliced thinly
1 head Garlic, peeled and minced
240 ml Vinegar
120 ml Soy Sauce
240 ml Water
2 Bay Leaves
To taste Salt and Pepper
Instructions
  1. In a wide pan over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic and cook until softened.
  2. Add pork and cook, turning as needed, until lightly browned. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 7 to 10 minutes.
  3. Add chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and juices run clear.
  4. Add vinegar and bring to a boil, uncovered and without stirring, for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Add soy sauce, water, and bay leaves. Continue to boil, skimming scum that floats on top.
  6. Lower heat, cover, and simmer until meat is tender and sauce is reduced.
  7. Adjust taste with salt and pepper as needed.

Bistek Tagalog
View recipe source here: Bistek Tagalog
Ingredients
1 kg Beef Sirloin or Tenderloin, thinly sliced
120 ml Soy Sauce
60 ml Calamansi Juice (or Lemon Juice)
1 tsp Ground Black Pepper
4 cloves Garlic, minced
2 Large Onions, sliced into rings
2 tbsp Cooking Oil
240 ml Water
To taste Salt
Instructions
  1. In a bowl, combine beef slices, soy sauce, calamansi juice, ground black pepper, and minced garlic. Mix well and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onion rings and saut√© until they become translucent. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of cooking oil. Add the marinated beef slices, reserving the marinade, and cook until browned.
  4. Pour in the reserved marinade and water. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 20 to 30 minutes, or until the beef is tender. Adjust the seasoning with salt if necessary.
  5. Once the beef is tender and the sauce has slightly thickened, add the saut√©ed onion rings back into the pan. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Serve hot over steamed rice. Enjoy!

Ginisang Monggo
View recipe source here: Ginisang Munggo at Chicharon
Ingredients
200 g Mung Beans (Monggo), rinsed and drained
1.5 liters Water
1 tbsp Vegetable Oil
1 Onion, peeled and sliced thinly
3 cloves Garlic, peeled and minced
2 Roma Tomatoes, chopped
250 g Pork Belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
1 tbsp Fish Sauce
100 g Chicharon (Pork Cracklings), crushed
2 cups Fresh Spinach Leaves
To taste Salt and Pepper
Instructions
  1. In a pot over medium heat, combine mung beans and water. Bring to a boil, skimming off any foam that floats on top. Lower heat, cover, and simmer for about 45 to 60 minutes or until the mung beans are softened and the skins have burst.
  2. In another pot over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic and saut√© until softened.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook, mashing with the back of a spoon, until softened.
  4. Add pork belly and cook until lightly browned.
  5. Add fish sauce and cook for about 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. Add cooked mung beans along with the cooking liquid. Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for about 10 to 15 minutes to allow flavors to meld. If the mixture is too thick, add additional water or broth to reach the desired consistency.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Add spinach leaves and cook until just wilted.
  9. Ladle into bowls and top with crushed chicharon. Serve hot.

Pork Sinigang
View recipe source here: Pork Sinigang
Ingredients
1 kg Pork Belly, cut into chunks
2 liters Water
1 Medium Onion, quartered
2 Roma Tomatoes, quartered
150 g Taro (Gabi), peeled and quartered
100 g Radish (Labanos), sliced
100 g String Beans (Sitaw), cut into 5 cm lengths
2 Green Finger Chilies (Siling Pangsigang)
1 bunch Water Spinach (Kangkong), trimmed
50 g Tamarind Paste or Sinigang Mix
2 tbsp Fish Sauce (Patis)
To taste Salt and Pepper
Instructions
  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork belly and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, skimming off any scum that rises to the surface.
  2. Add onions and tomatoes. Cook for about 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add taro and cook until tender.
  4. Add radish and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Add string beans and green chilies. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Stir in tamarind paste or sinigang mix and fish sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Add water spinach, stir, and remove from heat. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Filipino Fried Chicken
View recipe source here: Filipino Fried Chicken
Ingredients
1.5 kg Whole Chicken, cut into serving pieces
1 liter Water
1 Medium Onion, quartered
3 cloves Garlic, peeled and smashed
2 tbsp Soy Sauce
1 tbsp Salt
1 tsp Ground Black Pepper
2 Bay Leaves
2 cups Cooking Oil (for frying)
To taste Salt and Pepper (for final seasoning)
Instructions
  1. In a large pot, combine chicken, water, onion, garlic, soy sauce, salt, pepper, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 20 minutes or until chicken is tender.
  2. Remove chicken from the pot and drain well. Let it cool for a few minutes.
  3. Heat cooking oil in a large skillet or deep fryer over medium heat. Fry chicken pieces in batches until golden and crispy.
  4. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil. Season with additional salt and pepper, if desired.
  5. Serve hot with steamed rice or a dipping sauce of your choice.

Bulalo
View recipe source here: Bulalo
Ingredients
1.5 kg Beef Shank, with bone marrow
2 liters Water
1 Large Onion, peeled and quartered
2 Corn Cobs, cut into chunks
200 g Green Beans
200 g Chinese Cabbage (or Napa Cabbage)
1 tbsp Fish Sauce
To taste Salt and Pepper
Instructions
  1. In a large pot, combine beef shanks, water, and onion. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Cook for 2 hours or until beef is tender, skimming off scum as needed.
  2. Add corn and continue to simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  4. Add green beans and cabbage. Cook for 2 minutes or until vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  5. Serve hot with steamed rice and a side of dipping sauce, if desired.

Leche Flan
View recipe source here: Leche Flan
Ingredients
10 pieces Egg Yolks
1 can (14 oz) Sweetened Condensed Milk
1 cup Fresh Milk or Evaporated Milk
1 cup Granulated Sugar
1 teaspoon Vanilla Extract
Instructions
  1. In a bowl, beat the egg yolks until smooth.
  2. Add the sweetened condensed milk, fresh milk, and vanilla extract. Mix well until fully combined.
  3. In a separate pan, melt the granulated sugar over low heat until it turns golden brown. Carefully pour the caramel into the bottom of a mold, swirling to coat evenly.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the caramelized sugar in the mold.
  5. Cover the mold with foil and steam over low heat for about 45 minutes, or until set.
  6. Allow to cool, then refrigerate for several hours before serving.

Halo-Halo
View recipe source here: Halo-Halo
Ingredients
1/4 cup Sweetened Red Beans
1/4 cup Sweetened White Beans
1 large Banana, thinly sliced
1/4 cup Jackfruit, sliced
1/4 cup Macapuno Strips
1/4 cup Nata de Coco
1/4 cup Red Palm Seeds (Kaong)
1 cup Shaved Ice
1/4 cup Evaporated Milk
1/4 cup Sweetened Condensed Milk
1 scoop Ube Ice Cream
Instructions
  1. In a tall glass, layer the sweetened red beans, sweetened white beans, sliced banana, jackfruit, macapuno strips, nata de coco, and red palm seeds.
  2. Add a generous amount of shaved ice on top of the layered ingredients.
  3. Pour the evaporated milk and sweetened condensed milk over the shaved ice.
  4. Top with a scoop of ube ice cream.
  5. Serve immediately, mixing all the ingredients together before eating.
